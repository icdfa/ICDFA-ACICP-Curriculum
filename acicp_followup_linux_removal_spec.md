# ACICP Follow-Up Redesign Specification

## User Request

The user clarified that ACICP is an **advanced programme** and asked that the remaining Linux courses also be removed.

## Additional Courses to Remove

1. **ACICP 103 – Linux Systems Engineering I**
2. **ACICP 104 – Linux Systems Engineering II**

## Curriculum Impact

After the earlier 6-month redesign, the public curriculum contained **15 published course sections** and **162 hands-on labs**. Removing ACICP 103 and ACICP 104 changes the public curriculum to:

- **13 published course sections**
- **123 hands-on labs**

## Revised Programme Framing

The programme remains a **24-week (6-month)** advanced curriculum, but it is now positioned as an infrastructure-security pathway for learners who already possess operating-system administration fundamentals.

## Revised Phase Structure

The programme will remain at **7 phases** for continuity.

| Phase | Focus Area | Duration | Courses |
|---|---|---:|---|
| 1 | Cybersecurity Foundation | 2 weeks | ACICP 101 |
| 2 | Network & Security Operations | 4 weeks | ACICP 201-202 |
| 3 | Critical Infrastructure (ICS) | 4 weeks | ACICP 301-303 |
| 4 | Offensive Cyber Operations & Firewall Engineering | 5 weeks | ACICP 401-403 |
| 5 | Cloud Security | 2 weeks | ACICP 601 |
| 6 | Virtual Infrastructure | 3 weeks | ACICP 604 |
| 7 | Digital Forensics & Final Simulation | 4 weeks | ACICP 701-702 |

**Total Duration:** 24 weeks

## Document Update Rules

The repository should be updated to:

- Remove all ACICP 103 and ACICP 104 course sections from the main curriculum.
- Update course totals from **15** to **13** and lab totals from **162** to **123**.
- Reframe admission requirements as prior systems-administration or cybersecurity experience instead of Linux-course preparation.
- Remove Linux-course references from phase tables, FAQs, handbook summaries, and partnership mapping.
- Keep generic operating-system compatibility statements only where they describe device support rather than curriculum content.
