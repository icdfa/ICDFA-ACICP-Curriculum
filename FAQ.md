## Admission & Application

### Q1: What are the admission requirements for ACICP?

**A:** Applicants must have:

- Prior knowledge of cybersecurity fundamentals (equivalent to CompTIA Security+)
- Basic networking understanding (TCP/IP, DNS, HTTP/HTTPS)
- Prior systems administration or command-line experience
- Strong English language proficiency (written and verbal)
- Demonstrated commitment to cybersecurity and professional maturity

We also recommend having 2–5 years of IT or cybersecurity experience, though this is not mandatory.

---

### Q2: How do I apply to the ACICP?

**A:** To apply:

1. Visit the ICDFA website and click "Apply Now"
2. Complete the online application form with personal and professional information
3. Upload required documents (resume, transcripts, certifications)
4. Submit a 500-word essay on why you want to join ACICP
5. Schedule a technical assessment interview
6. Wait for admission decision (typically within 2–3 weeks)

All application materials must be submitted in English.

---

### Q3: What is the application deadline?

**A:** Application deadlines vary by cohort. Current deadlines:

- **Spring 2026 Cohort:** March 31, 2026
- **Summer 2026 Cohort:** June 30, 2026
- **Fall 2026 Cohort:** September 30, 2026
- **Winter 2027 Cohort:** December 31, 2026

Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis. Early applications are encouraged as cohorts fill quickly.

---

### Q4: What is the selection criteria for admission?

**A:** Admission decisions are based on:

- Technical competency assessment (40%)
- Professional experience and background (25%)
- Demonstrated commitment to cybersecurity (20%)
- Communication skills and English proficiency (10%)
- Ability to work in high-pressure environments (5%)

We look for candidates who show both technical capability and professional maturity.

---

### Q5: What if I don't meet all admission requirements?

**A:** Consider our **Fast-Track Bridge Course**, a 2-week intensive module designed to prepare you for the main ACICP. The Bridge Course covers:

- Cybersecurity fundamentals
- Networking essentials (TCP/IP)
- Systems administration fundamentals
- Hands-on lab skills

Upon successful completion of the Bridge Course (≥70%), you are guaranteed admission to the main ACICP.

---

### Q6: How many fellows are admitted per cohort?

**A:** Maximum **50 fellows per cohort** to ensure:

- Personalized instructor attention
- Adequate lab resources and equipment
- Effective peer collaboration
- Quality of training and outcomes

Cohorts are highly competitive, and we recommend applying early.

---

### Q7: Can I defer my admission to a later cohort?

**A:** Yes, you can defer your admission to the next available cohort (typically 3 months later). Deferrals must be requested in writing to the ICDFA Admissions Office within 2 weeks of receiving your admission decision.

---

### Q8: Is there an application fee?

**A:** No, there is no application fee. However, upon admission, a **commitment fee of $10 USD** (for scholarship track) or **$150 USD** (for standard track) is required to secure your spot in the cohort.

## Programme Details & Schedule

### Q9: How long is the ACICP?

**A:** The ACICP is a **24-week (6-month) intensive programme** divided into **2 phases**:

- Phase 1: Core Security, Network Operations & Critical Infrastructure (11 weeks)
- Phase 2: Offensive Operations, Cloud Security & Digital Forensics (13 weeks)

---

### Q10: What is the schedule? Can I study part-time?

**A:** The ACICP is **full-time only**:

- **Hours:** 8 hours per day, 5 days per week (Monday–Friday)
- **Schedule:** 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM (with 1-hour lunch break)
- **Additional Study:** 10+ hours per week of self-study and preparation
- **Total Commitment:** 50+ hours per week

Part-time study is **not available**. The programme is designed as an intensive, full-time commitment.

---

### Q11: What are the start dates for upcoming cohorts?

**A:** Cohort start dates:

- **Spring 2026:** April 6, 2026
- **Summer 2026:** July 6, 2026
- **Fall 2026:** October 5, 2026
- **Winter 2027:** January 4, 2027

All cohorts run for exactly 24 weeks (6 months) in a compressed intensive delivery format.

---

### Q12: Can I take a break or pause the programme?

**A:** No, the ACICP does not allow breaks or pauses once you begin. The programme is designed as a continuous, intensive experience. If you need to withdraw, you must do so before the programme starts. Withdrawals after the start date result in forfeiture of tuition.

---

### Q13: What courses are included in the ACICP?

**A:** The ACICP now presents **12 published course sections** across **2 phases**:

**Phase 1:** ACICP 101, ACICP 201, ACICP 202, ACICP 301, ACICP 302, and ACICP 303

## Technical Requirements

### Q15: What are the technical prerequisites?

**A:** You should have:

- Prior systems administration or command-line proficiency in enterprise environments
- Understanding of TCP/IP networking (IP addresses, ports, protocols)
- Familiarity with cybersecurity concepts (firewalls, encryption, authentication)
- Experience with at least one programming language (Python, Bash, or similar)
- Ability to use virtualization software (VirtualBox, VMware, or Hyper-V)

If you lack any of these skills, the **Fast-Track Bridge Course** will prepare you.

---

### Q16: What hardware do I need?

**A:** Minimum hardware requirements:

- **Laptop/Desktop:** Intel i5/i7 or AMD Ryzen 5/7 (or equivalent)
- **RAM:** 16 GB minimum (32 GB recommended)
- **Storage:** 512 GB SSD (1 TB recommended)
- **Network:** Stable internet connection (25+ Mbps recommended)
- **OS:** Windows, macOS, or Linux

ICDFA provides cloud-based lab environments, so you don't need enterprise-grade hardware, but your machine must be capable of running virtualization software.

---

### Q17: What software do I need to install?

**A:** Required software:

- **Virtualization:** VirtualBox (free) or VMware (licensed)
- **Terminal/SSH:** PuTTY, Terminal, or iTerm2
- **Text Editor:** VS Code, Sublime Text, or similar
- **Browser:** Chrome, Firefox, or Edge (latest version)
- **VPN:** OpenVPN or similar (provided by ICDFA)

All other tools and platforms are provided through ICDFA labs or partner platforms (Microsoft Azure, AWS, Cisco, NDG NETLAB+).

---

### Q18: Do I need to purchase any software licenses?

**A:** No. All required software is either:

- **Free/Open Source:** VirtualBox, VS Code, Wireshark, Metasploit, etc.
- **Provided by ICDFA:** Lab environments, security tools, platforms
- **Provided by Partners:** Microsoft Azure, AWS, Cisco Academy, NDG NETLAB+

No additional software purchases are required.

---

### Q19: What internet speed do I need?

**A:** Minimum **25 Mbps** download and **10 Mbps** upload. However, we recommend:

- **50+ Mbps** download for smooth video streaming and large file transfers
- **Stable, low-latency connection** (wired Ethernet preferred over WiFi)
- **Backup internet option** in case of primary connection failure

Lab environments are cloud-based, so a reliable internet connection is essential.

---

### Q20: Can I use a Mac or Linux laptop?

**A:** Yes, absolutely. The ACICP is platform-agnostic and works on:

- **Windows** (10 or later)
- **macOS** (10.14 or later)
- **Linux** (Ubuntu 20.04 or later)

Lab environments span cloud, network, industrial, and forensic simulation platforms, so any supported laptop is acceptable. All instructors support all platforms.

## Labs & Hands-On Training

### Q27: How many hours per week are spent in labs?

**A:** Approximately **30–35 hours per week** are spent in hands-on labs:

- **Guided Labs:** 20–25 hours (instructor-led exercises)
- **Independent Labs:** 5–10 hours (self-paced practice)
- **Team Labs:** 5 hours (collaborative group exercises)
- **Capstone Labs:** 10–15 hours (final phase only)

---

### Q28: What lab environments are used?

**A:** Lab environments include:

- **Cloud Platforms:** Microsoft Azure, AWS, Google Cloud

### Q31: Are labs graded?

## Assessment & Grading

### Q36: How is my performance assessed?

**A:** Assessment includes:

- **Continuous Lab Performance (40%):** Weekly lab submissions and evaluations
- **Mid-Programme Exam (20%):** Week 12 comprehensive exam
- **Final Lab Assessment (20%):** Week 23 practical evaluation
- **Capstone Project & CTF (20%):** Week 24 live simulation and competition

**Passing score: ≥70% overall**

---

### Q37: What is the "No Retake Policy"?

**A:** Final assessments (Mid-Programme Exam, Final Lab Assessment, Capstone) are **one attempt only**:

- You cannot retake final assessments
- Failure results in programme non-completion
- No partial credit or makeup opportunities
- Continuous lab performance can be improved throughout the programme

This policy ensures rigorous standards and genuine mastery.

---

### Q38: What happens if I fail an assessment?

**A:** If you fail a final assessment:

- You will not receive the CCICP credential
- You cannot retake the assessment
- You will not be eligible for job placement assistance
- You may reapply for a future cohort (after 6 months)

If you fail continuous lab performance, you will receive a warning and remedial training. Three consecutive weeks of underperformance (below 70%) results in expulsion.

---

### Q39: What is the grading scale?

**A:** ACICP uses the following grading scale:

| Grade | Percentage | Status |
|:------|:-----------|:-------|
| A | 90–100% | Excellent |
| B | 80–89% | Good |
| C | 70–79% | Satisfactory |
| F | Below 70% | Fail |

To graduate, you must achieve a minimum grade of C (70%) overall.

---

### Q40: Can I see my grades during the programme?

**A:** Yes, grades are available:

- **Weekly:** Lab submission grades posted within 48 hours
- **Real-time:** Access to your grade dashboard through the ACICP Student Portal
- **Feedback:** Detailed feedback provided by instructors for all assessments
- **Progress Reports:** Monthly progress reports sent to all trainees

## Policies & Conduct

### Q41: What is the Strict Lab Discipline Policy?

**A:** The Strict Lab Discipline Policy prohibits:

- Unauthorized access or modification of systems
- Malicious activity (creating malware, launching attacks outside labs)
- Intellectual property violations (copying/sharing materials)
- Professional misconduct (dishonesty, harassment, discrimination)

**Consequences:** Written warning → Suspension (1–4 weeks) → Expulsion (permanent)

Critical violations (attacks on external systems) result in immediate expulsion.

---

### Q42: What is the Community Development Service (CDS)?

**A:** All trainees must complete **20 hours of CDS** during the 24-week programme:

- **Cybersecurity Awareness (10 hours):** Workshops, training, mentoring
- **Institutional/Community Service (10 hours):** ICDFA support, incident response, outreach

CDS completion is required for graduation. Failure to complete CDS results in programme non-completion.

---

### Q43: What is the Code of Conduct?

**A:** All trainees must maintain:

- **Integrity:** Honesty in all academic and professional interactions
- **Respect:** Courtesy toward instructors, peers, and staff
- **Responsibility:** Ownership of learning and actions
- **Confidentiality:** Protection of sensitive information
- **Collaboration:** Effective teamwork while maintaining academic integrity

Violations may result in warnings, suspension, or expulsion.

---

### Q44: What is the confidentiality agreement?

**A:** Upon admission, you sign a confidentiality agreement prohibiting disclosure of:

- Lab scenarios and exercises
- Assessment questions and answers
- Proprietary tools and exploits
- Information about other trainees
- ICDFA operational procedures

**Breach of confidentiality may result in legal action and damages.**

---

### Q45: Can I share lab materials with others?

**A:** No. Lab materials are the exclusive property of ICDFA. Sharing materials with non-programme participants is prohibited and may result in:

- Disciplinary action (warning, suspension, expulsion)
- Legal action for intellectual property violation
- Forfeiture of tuition and credentials

## Technical Issues & Troubleshooting

### Q61: What if I can't access the lab environment?

**A:** Troubleshooting steps:

1. Check your internet connection (25+ Mbps recommended)
2. Clear your browser cache and cookies
3. Try a different browser (Chrome, Firefox, Edge)
4. Restart your computer and router
5. Contact technical support if issues persist

**Technical Support:** studentsupport@icdfa.edu.ng 

---

### Q62: What if my lab environment crashes?

**A:** If your lab crashes:

1. **Immediately contact technical support** (provide your lab ID and timestamp)
2. Technical support will investigate and restore your environment
3. If data was lost, backups are available
4. You will receive an extension on lab deadlines if needed

Lab crashes are rare, but we have backup systems in place.

---

### Q63: What if I forget my password?

**A:** To reset your password:

1. Go to the ACICP Student Portal login page
2. Click "Forgot Password"
3. Enter your email address
4. Follow the password reset link sent to your email
5. Create a new password and log in

If you don't receive the reset email, contact Student Services.

---

### Q64: What if I have connectivity issues during a lab exam?

**A:** If you lose connectivity during an exam:

1. **Reconnect as soon as possible** (your work is auto-saved)
2. **Contact technical support immediately** with your exam ID
3. **Document the issue** (screenshot, timestamp, duration)
4. Technical support will review the incident and may grant an extension

Connectivity issues are taken seriously, and accommodations will be made.

---

### Q65: What if my computer doesn't meet the hardware requirements?

**A:** If your hardware is below minimum specs:

1. Contact Student Services to discuss your situation
2. Consider upgrading your hardware (budget-friendly options available)

4. Explore cloud-based alternatives (remote desktop access)

We want to ensure you have the tools needed to succeed.
