## Welcome to ACICP

Congratulations on your acceptance into the **Advanced Critical Infrastructure Cybersecurity Programme (ACICP)**. You have been selected as one of an elite cohort of cybersecurity professionals committed to defending critical national infrastructure.

This handbook outlines the expectations, policies, and procedures that govern your participation in the programme. It is your responsibility to read, understand, and comply with all policies contained herein.

**Key Principle:** The ACICP is designed for serious, committed, and disciplined trainees only. There are **no shortcuts**, **no extensions**, and **no compromises**. Only those who demonstrate competence, resilience, and discipline will successfully complete the programme.

---

## Programme Overview

### Programme Structure

The ACICP is a **36-week (9-month) intensive training programme** divided into **8 Phases** covering **18 specialized courses** and **100+ hands-on labs**.

| Phase | Focus Area | Duration | Courses |
|:------|:-----------|:---------|:--------|
| 1 | Core Systems & Security | 4 weeks | ACICP 101–104 |
| 2 | Network & Security Operations | 2 weeks | ACICP 201–202 |
| 3 | Critical Infrastructure (ICS) | 3 weeks | ACICP 301–303 |
| 4 | Offensive Cyber Operations | 3 weeks | ACICP 401–403 |
| 5 | Defensive Security | 3 weeks | ACICP 501–503 |
| 6 | Cloud Security | 2 weeks | ACICP 601–602 |
| 7 | Virtual Infrastructure | 4 weeks | ACICP 603–604 |
| 8 | Digital Forensics & Capstone | 4 weeks | ACICP 701–702 |

### Training Delivery

The programme is delivered through:

- **Instructor-led classroom sessions** (8 hours per day, 5 days per week)
- **Hands-on lab exercises** (100+ practical labs with real infrastructure)
- **Live cyber range simulations** (24-hour CTF in final phase)
- **Peer collaboration** (team-based exercises and group projects)

### Expected Time Commitment

Trainees are expected to dedicate **50+ hours per week** to the programme, including:

- 40 hours of classroom and lab time
- 10+ hours of self-study and preparation
- Additional hours for assessments and capstone project

## Admission Requirements

### Minimum Qualifications

Applicants must have:

- Prior knowledge of cybersecurity fundamentals (equivalent to CompTIA Security+)
- Basic networking understanding (TCP/IP, DNS, HTTP/HTTPS)
- Familiarity with Linux systems (command-line basics)
- Strong English language proficiency (written and verbal)

### Selection Criteria

Admission decisions are based on:

- Technical competency assessment
- Demonstrated commitment to cybersecurity
- Professional maturity and discipline
- Ability to work in a high-pressure environment

### Cohort Size

- **Maximum 50 fellows per cohort** to ensure personalized attention and hands-on lab access
- Cohorts are formed quarterly

## Strict Lab Discipline Policy

### Overview

The ACICP operates under a **zero-tolerance discipline policy** for lab misconduct. Labs simulate real critical infrastructure environments, and unauthorized actions can have serious consequences.

### Prohibited Actions

The following actions are strictly prohibited and will result in immediate sanctions:

#### 1. Unauthorized Access or Modification

- Accessing systems or data beyond assigned lab scope
- Modifying lab configurations without instructor approval
- Attempting to bypass security controls or authentication mechanisms
- Accessing other trainees' accounts or data

#### 2. Malicious Activity

- Launching attacks against systems outside the designated lab environment
- Creating or deploying malware or exploits without explicit authorization
- Attempting to disrupt lab infrastructure or services
- Sharing exploits or tools outside the programme

#### 3. Intellectual Property Violations

- Copying or distributing proprietary lab materials
- Sharing lab solutions or answers with non-programme participants
- Using programme materials for unauthorized purposes
- Plagiarizing code or documentation

#### 4. Professional Misconduct

- Dishonesty or deception in assessments
- Impersonating instructors or administrators
- Harassment or discrimination of fellow trainees
- Violation of confidentiality agreements

### Consequences of Violations

Violations of the Strict Lab Discipline Policy result in the following escalating sanctions:

| Violation Level | First Offense | Second Offense | Third Offense |
|:----------------|:--------------|:---------------|:--------------|
| Minor (unauthorized access to non-critical systems) | Written warning + remedial training | 1-week suspension | Expulsion |
| Major (malicious activity, IP violations) | 1-week suspension + remedial training | 2-week suspension | Expulsion |
| Critical (attacks on external systems, harassment) | Immediate expulsion | N/A | N/A |

**Note:** Expulsion is permanent and non-reversible. No refunds are provided.

### Monitoring & Enforcement

- All lab activities are logged and monitored
- Instructors review logs daily for suspicious activity
- Automated alerts trigger for policy violations
- Investigations are conducted by the ICDFA Disciplinary Committee

## No Retake Policy

### Assessment Structure

The ACICP includes the following assessments:

1. **Initial Lab Assessment** (Week 1)
2. **Continuous Lab Performance** (Weeks 2–35)
3. **Mid-Programme Competency Exam** (Week 18)
4. **Final Lab Assessment** (Week 35)
5. **Capstone Project & 24-Hour CTF** (Week 36)

### No Retake Rule

**Final assessments (Mid-Programme Exam, Final Lab Assessment, and Capstone) are ONE ATTEMPT ONLY.**

- Trainees who fail a final assessment cannot retake it
- Failure results in programme termination and non-completion status
- No partial credit or makeup opportunities are provided

### Continuous Assessment

Continuous lab performance (Weeks 2–35) is monitored throughout the programme. Trainees who fall below a **70% competency threshold** at any point will receive:

1. **Week 1 of underperformance:** Formal warning and remedial training plan
2. **Week 2 of underperformance:** Probationary status and daily check-ins with instructor
3. **Week 3 of underperformance:** Expulsion from the programme

### Passing Criteria

To successfully complete the ACICP, trainees must:

- Achieve **≥70% on the Mid-Programme Exam**
- Achieve **≥70% on the Final Lab Assessment**
- Achieve **≥70% on the Capstone Project & CTF**
- Maintain **≥70% average on continuous lab performance**
- Complete **all mandatory Community Development Service (CDS) requirements**

## Community Development Service (CDS)

### Overview

All ACICP trainees are required to participate in **Community Development Service (CDS)** activities. CDS is designed to foster a sense of responsibility to the broader cybersecurity community and society.

### CDS Requirements

Each trainee must complete **minimum 20 hours** of CDS during the 36-week programme, distributed as follows:

- **Cybersecurity Awareness Initiatives** (10 hours minimum)
  - Conducting cybersecurity awareness workshops in schools or community centers
  - Developing cybersecurity training materials for non-technical audiences
  - Participating in national cybersecurity awareness campaigns
  - Mentoring junior cybersecurity professionals

- **Institutional or Community Service** (10 hours minimum)
  - Contributing to ICDFA infrastructure and operations
  - Assisting with programme administration or logistics
  - Volunteering for cybersecurity incident response support
  - Participating in community outreach events

### CDS Verification

- Trainees must log all CDS hours in the ICDFA portal
- Instructors verify CDS completion with external organizations
- Failure to complete CDS requirements results in programme non-completion

## Assessment Structure

### Grading Scale

| Grade | Percentage | Status |
|:------|:-----------|:-------|
| A | 90–100% | Excellent |
| B | 80–89% | Good |
| C | 70–79% | Satisfactory |
| F | Below 70% | Fail |

### Assessment Components

#### 1. Initial Lab Assessment (Week 1)

- **Purpose:** Baseline competency evaluation
- **Format:** Practical lab exercises
- **Weight:** Diagnostic (not counted toward final grade)
- **Retake:** Unlimited (diagnostic only)

#### 2. Continuous Lab Performance (Weeks 2–35)

- **Purpose:** Monitor ongoing competency and engagement
- **Format:** Weekly lab submissions, code reviews, and instructor evaluations
- **Weight:** 40% of final grade
- **Retake:** Not applicable (continuous evaluation)
- **Threshold:** Must maintain ≥70% average

#### 3. Mid-Programme Competency Exam (Week 18)

- **Purpose:** Evaluate mastery of Phase 1–3 content
- **Format:** Written exam + practical lab scenarios
- **Weight:** 20% of final grade
- **Retake:** **NO RETAKES ALLOWED**
- **Passing Score:** ≥70%

#### 4. Final Lab Assessment (Week 35)

- **Purpose:** Comprehensive evaluation of all technical skills
- **Format:** Practical lab exercises covering all 8 phases
- **Weight:** 20% of final grade
- **Retake:** **NO RETAKES ALLOWED**
- **Passing Score:** ≥70%

#### 5. Capstone Project & 24-Hour CTF (Week 36)

- **Purpose:** Demonstrate mastery through real-world simulation
- **Format:** Live attack simulation + incident investigation + CTF competition
- **Weight:** 20% of final grade
- **Retake:** **NO RETAKES ALLOWED**
- **Passing Score:** ≥70%

### Final Grade Calculation

```
Final Grade = (Continuous Lab Performance × 0.40) 
            + (Mid-Programme Exam × 0.20) 
            + (Final Lab Assessment × 0.20) 
            + (Capstone Project & CTF × 0.20)
```

**Minimum passing grade: 70%**

## Intellectual Property & Confidentiality

### Lab Materials & Curriculum

All ACICP lab materials, curriculum, documentation, and tools are the exclusive property of the ICDFA. Trainees are granted a **limited, non-exclusive license** to use these materials solely for the purpose of completing the programme.

**Prohibited uses include:**

- Copying or reproducing materials without permission
- Sharing materials with non-programme participants
- Using materials for commercial purposes
- Distributing materials on public platforms

### Confidentiality Agreement

All trainees must sign a **Confidentiality Agreement** upon admission. This agreement prohibits disclosure of:

- Lab scenarios and exercises
- Assessment questions and answers
- Proprietary tools and exploits
- Information about other trainees
- ICDFA operational procedures

**Breach of confidentiality may result in legal action and damages.**

### Capstone Project Ownership

Capstone projects and research conducted during the programme remain the property of the ICDFA. Trainees may not publish or present capstone work without explicit written permission from the ICDFA.

## Grievance Procedures

### Informal Resolution

If you have a concern or complaint, first attempt to resolve it informally:

1. **Speak with your instructor** within 5 business days of the incident
2. **Document the conversation** (date, time, attendees, discussion)
3. **Allow 5 business days** for the instructor to respond

### Formal Grievance

If informal resolution is unsuccessful, file a formal grievance:

1. **Submit a written complaint** to the ICDFA Grievance Committee within 10 business days of the incident
2. **Include:** Date, description, parties involved, resolution sought, and supporting documentation
3. **The Committee will investigate** within 15 business days
4. **You will receive a written decision** within 20 business days

### Appeals

If you disagree with the Grievance Committee's decision, you may appeal to the ICDFA Executive Director within 10 business days. The Executive Director's decision is final.

### Retaliation Protection

The ICDFA prohibits retaliation against any trainee who files a grievance in good faith. Retaliation is grounds for disciplinary action against the responsible party.
